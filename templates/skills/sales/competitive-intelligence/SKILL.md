# Competitive Intelligence

Research competitors and generate actionable battlecards for sales conversations. Creates interactive HTML battlecards with comparison matrices, talk tracks, and landmine questions.

## How It Works

**Always (via web search)**:
- Deep-dive into competitor products, features, and pricing
- Track recent releases (last 90 days)
- Document your own product releases and differentiators
- Create side-by-side comparison matrices
- Develop sales talk tracks and objection responses
- Generate landmine questions to expose competitor weaknesses

**Enhanced (with connected tools)**:
- CRM: Win/loss patterns and deal-level competitor tracking
- Docs: Existing battlecards and competitive playbooks
- Chat: Field intelligence from internal teams
- Transcripts: Competitor mentions and customer objections

## Output Format

Self-contained HTML artifact featuring:

### Comparison Matrix
Overview grid comparing you against all competitors on key dimensions.

### Per-Competitor Battlecard
For each competitor:
- **Company Profile**: Overview, size, funding, target market
- **Positioning**: How they describe themselves, key claims
- **Recent Releases**: Last 90 days of product updates
- **Where They Win**: Honest assessment of their strengths
- **Where We Win**: Your differentiators with proof points
- **Pricing Intelligence**: Known pricing models and comparison
- **Talk Tracks**: Scenario-based responses for sales conversations
- **Objection Handling**: Common objections and recommended responses
- **Landmine Questions**: Questions that expose their weaknesses
- **Trap Questions**: Questions they set for you, with prepared responses

### Your Company Card
Your recent releases, differentiators, and proof points.

## Triggers

- "competitive intel on [competitor]"
- "how do we compare to [competitor]?"
- "battlecard for [competitor]"
- "what's new with [competitor]?"
- "research [competitor] vs us"

## Refresh Cadence

- Monthly for active competitors
- Before major deals
- After win/loss analysis
- After competitor announcements

## Principles

- Be honest about competitor strengths — credibility matters
- Focus on customer value, not feature lists
- Back claims with evidence (customer quotes, data, third-party validation)
- Keep it actionable for sales conversations
