# Draft Outreach

Create personalized cold outreach messages through research-first methodology. Research the prospect before drafting to ensure messages are personalized, not generic.

## How It Works

1. **Research**: Web search (+ optional enrichment/CRM data) to understand the prospect
2. **Draft**: Personalized message based on research findings
3. **Deliver**: Via email, LinkedIn, or text output

## Personalization Hierarchy

Priority order for personalization hooks:
1. **Trigger events**: Funding, hiring, product launches, leadership changes
2. **Mutual connections**: Shared contacts, communities, or experiences
3. **Their content**: Articles, posts, or talks they've published
4. **Company initiatives**: Strategic projects or public priorities
5. **Role-based pain points**: Common challenges for their title/function

## Output Format

### Research Summary
Key findings about the prospect and company, with relevance to your offering.

### Email Draft

**Subject line options** (3 alternatives):
1. [Trigger-based subject]
2. [Value-based subject]
3. [Curiosity-based subject]

**Email body**: AIDA framework (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action)

### LinkedIn Alternative
- Connection request message (300 char limit)
- Follow-up message template

### Reasoning
Why this approach was chosen and what research supported it.

### Follow-up Sequence (optional)
- Day 3: [Follow-up angle]
- Day 7: [Different value add]
- Day 14: [Final touch]

## Style Rules

- **Plain text only** — no markdown asterisks, bold, or fancy formatting in emails
- **Short paragraphs** — 1-2 sentences each
- **No generic openers** — avoid "I hope this email finds you well"
- **One clear CTA** — single, specific ask
- **No feature dumps** — focus on one relevant value proposition
- **Conversational tone** — write like a human, not a marketing machine
- **Under 150 words** for initial outreach

## Message Types

### Cold Outreach
Research-backed first touch with specific personalization hook.

### Warm Reconnection
Reference prior relationship, shared context, or mutual connection.

### Post-Event Follow-up
Reference specific conversation or session from the event.

### Referral Introduction
Leverage the mutual connection with appropriate context.

## Tips

- Include the prospect's domain for more precise research
- Specify the person's name and title for targeted personalization
- State your goal ("book a demo", "start a conversation", "share a resource")
- Mention any specific trigger events you know about
